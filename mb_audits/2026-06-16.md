## Recommendation

**Fix the mb_match export/push pipeline — it is now the binding constraint on the entire activation mandate, not any single market.**

The Matchbook scanner exited 0 on Jun 16 (per `last_routine`), yet no `2026-06-16.json` was produced. This is the **second mb_match absence in three days** (Jun 14, Jun 16) and the third in the recent window — **40% of the last five days have no cross-exchange data.** The failure sits downstream of a healthy MB scan, so the most likely culprits are the mb_match aggregation/export step or the 03:00 UTC push, not the scrape itself.

This matters now specifically because the Jun 15 report identified the **first World Cup `MATCH_ODDS` same-type match** (Saudi Arabia v Uruguay, +0.20 MB pricing edge) — the strongest activation signal in the series — and flagged that it needed **2–3 consecutive days of confirmation** to rule out a one-off. Jun 16 was the first of those confirmation days, and it is missing. With the World Cup group stage producing exactly the daily soccer volume the bot's primary Match Odds signal needs, **every missed day during the tournament is an irrecoverable gap in the activation evidence base.**

Concretely: instrument the mb_match generation/push step to log success/failure separately from the scanner exit code, and alert on a missing daily file, so the operator learns of a gap at 03:00 rather than the analyst discovering it at 08:00. **No MB activation decision should be made while data continuity is this unreliable** — the readiness picture cannot be validated on a 60%-complete dataset.
