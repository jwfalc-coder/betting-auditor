### F002 — BF cross-reference market-scope mismatch: Brazil 71.5pp spurious confirmation

At the 2300 run, the analyzer logged: `BF cross-ref: Brazil MB=76.3% BF=9.5% claude=81.0% bf_edge=71.5pp confirms=True`. This entry is structurally inconsistent with a valid cross-reference: a 66.8pp gap between MB (76.3%) and BF (9.5%) for the same runner cannot reflect the same market. The most plausible explanation is that Matchbook's "Brazil" runner is the head-to-head match odds (Brazil vs some opponent in a friendly or Nations League qualifier — typical match-odds probability range for Brazil is 55–80%), while Betfair's "Brazil" entry in the outright index is a tournament winner market (e.g., Copa América, or a multi-team competition where Brazil's outright probability is 8–15%). The cross-reference matcher is matching on runner name without validating that the underlying market type or competition scope is comparable.

The confirms=True result in this instance caused no damage — the 3 Winner back signals were already blocked by matchbook_no_backs. However, the structural risk is that an identically misconfigured cross-ref could fire for a lay signal or a Match Odds/Moneyline back and produce a confirms=True that:
- Avoids the stake-halving that would apply if confirms=False
- Provides false confidence that the signal is externally validated

The same structural issue was identified on May 21 (Brentford/Sunderland outright vs top-6 finish mismatch) and flagged as requiring a market-type compatibility filter. The Brazil/Portugal instances on May 25 confirm this is a recurring pattern for international football, where the same runner name appears in both head-to-head and tournament-winner markets across Matchbook and Betfair simultaneously.

**From the May 26 daily_snapshot.json last_routine** (the 0600 May 26 run, captured for context): `BF cross-ref: Portugal MB=63.3% BF=8.0% claude=73.3% bf_edge=65.3pp confirms=True` — same pattern. Portugal at 63.3% on Matchbook (match odds) vs 8.0% on Betfair (tournament outright). The next day's run also shows `BF cross-ref: Arsenal MB=42.7% BF=42.7% claude=47.2% bf_edge=4.5pp confirms=False` and `BF disagrees: Arsenal back — stake will be halved by paper_tracker` — this is an example of a correct cross-ref match (both platforms have Arsenal at 42.7%, small bf_edge, correctly produces confirms=False and stake halving). The disparity between the correctly-behaving Arsenal entry and the misconfigured Brazil/Portugal entries confirms the issue is specific to name-based matching across different market scopes, not a global cross-ref failure.
