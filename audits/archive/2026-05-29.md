### F002 — Dual positions on De Minaur/Mensik: correlated exposure risk

The combined -£7.60 loss from a single match result (Mensik beats De Minaur) reflects a structural gap: two positions placed in separate runs on opposite runners of the same two-outcome match. In a two-runner Match Odds market, a BACK on Runner A is economically equivalent to a LAY against Runner B — both are bets on Runner A winning. The two positions created a doubled directional bet on De Minaur, and when Mensik upset De Minaur (0-6, 6-2, 6-2, 6-3 in the third round — starting with De Minaur dominating before a remarkable Mensik recovery), both lost simultaneously.

The dedup logic correctly prevented same-direction duplicate positions ("Skipping (already open): Winner BACK") but this check is run-to-run for the SAME runner/market. It does not evaluate whether a new LAY on Runner B is equivalent to an existing BACK on Runner A in the same two-outcome market.

The positions were individually valid signals (both ≥15pp edge, both on Betfair Match Odds), but their combination amplified single-match exposure to £7.60 net. This is a medium-confidence finding (one confirmed instance), but the mechanism is structurally predictable wherever the researcher consecutively detects edge on both sides of a high-profile two-runner match.
