### F001 — Brentford/Sunderland cross-ref market type mismatch confirmed

The 1000 run cross-ref entries for Brentford (MB=29.4%, BF=97.1%, bf_edge=85.9pp, confirms=True) and Sunderland (MB=23.8%, BF=96.2%, bf_edge=87.1pp, confirms=True) were noted as a "notable, not a formal finding" in the May 20 audit narrative. Today I'm elevating this to a formal finding with external evidence:

Both Brentford and Sunderland are Premier League clubs in 2025-26. Brentford are a safe mid-table PL side; Sunderland were promoted from the Championship for this season. With the 2025-26 PL relegation battle down to Wolves (confirmed relegated), Burnley (confirmed relegated), and one of Spurs/West Ham (decided on May 24's final matchday), both Brentford and Sunderland are safe from relegation — consistent with near-100% "Not Relegated" Betfair prices.

The cross-ref validator is matching the team name "Brentford" from a Matchbook "Top X Finish" market (at 29.4% probability) against a Betfair "Not To Be Relegated" market (at 97.1%). These are structurally incompatible questions. The resulting 85.9pp bf_edge and confirms=True is meaningless — it does not validate the Matchbook signal at all.

The operational impact is currently zero (the Brentford/Sunderland Claude probabilities of 11.2% and 9.1% are well below the 12pp threshold, so no paper trades result from these cross-refs). But the same matching error would give false confirmations for any future runner whose name appears in the Betfair cross-ref index under a different market type. The fix is a market-type compatibility filter in the cross-ref lookup.

### F002 — Zero new trades; 'blocked MB market type' filter observed

May 21 was a quiet day for execution: all signals generated were blocked before trade logging. The high-odds lay cap (4.0 max, correctly blocking Top 6 Finish at 4.20) and the already-open dedup (Top 6 Finish, Top 4 Finish) are both documented behaviors operating correctly.

This indicates a per-market-type edge ceiling — trades in "Top 4 Finish" markets on Matchbook are blocked if the claimed edge is >= 25.0pp. This is not documented in CLAUDE.md's threshold section. In this instance the block was redundant (the already-open dedup would have fired anyway), but the filter is a live circuit-breaker on high-edge outright finish signals.

Given that the bot's model was calibrated primarily on Betfair political lay bets and shows documented positive-narrative bias in research output (improvement_inputs.json boost words: 'strong' ×83, 'won' ×75), a 25pp edge ceiling on outright finish markets is a sensible defensive measure. It's worth documenting this formally so it's not treated as unexpected behavior in future audits.
