A 28.77pp LAY on Hillier implies the model assessed his win probability at roughly (market_probability - 28.77pp). At typical head-to-head odds this might mean the model initially assessed Hillier at ~20-30% win probability, then later at ~60-70%. A 30-40pp swing in a head-to-head market is extraordinary unless there was a major external development (injury, weather, field change) between placements.

The broader implication: if the model can generate conflicting LAY and BACK signals on the same selection in the same market across runs, both passing the dedup filter, then the risk of a perfect offsetting scenario (both signals surviving to settlement, one winning and one losing at full stake) is structural and repeatable. The -£0.86 net is acceptable in isolation; a future instance where both positions are at full stake (e.g., a LAY at 4.5 max odds and a BACK at high stake) could generate more meaningful losses.
