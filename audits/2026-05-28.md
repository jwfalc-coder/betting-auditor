### F001 — Sub-floor paper trade at 3.36pp (new finding)

The 1400 run logged a Match Odds BACK paper trade at 3.36pp edge. This is the single most notable operational finding today. Three aspects deserve attention:

**1. Edge floor breach:** The 10pp hard floor is specified in CLAUDE.md as absolute. The analyzer reported 4 signals at 10pp+ for the 1400 run — yet the paper_tracker logged a trade at 3.36pp. The trade either:
- Was passed through by a probability-based bypass (the distribution_logger classed the same trade as "near_certainty" based on its 87.72% implied probability, suggesting a separate near-certainty processing path exists in the code)
- OR reached the paper_tracker via a code path that doesn't check edge against the 10pp floor

**2. Band classification mismatch:** The distribution_logger correctly put the trade in "near_certainty" (6 total/4 resolved after logging, then 5 resolved after settlement). However, the paper tracker's by_band snapshot classifies it in "7-9pp" (the band gained exactly 1 entry with the Madison Keys settlement's +£0.66). This is an internal inconsistency: the same trade is categorized differently across two subsystems.

**3. Outcome neutrality:** The trade WIN (+£0.66) is irrelevant to the principle — the hard floor exists to guard against model calibration error at low-edge signals. At 87.72% probability and 3.36pp edge, the model's true probability estimate would be 91.08%. An error of 3pp in the model (giving 88% instead of 91%) would make this a negative EV bet after overround. Near-certainty backs have asymmetric downside: the profit is bounded (small stake on 1.14 odds) but the failure mode (model overconfidence on high-probability events) is exactly where calibration error tends to surface.
