### F001 — Potential resolver incorrect settlement: Russell Henley Top 20 and Top 10 Finish LAYs

This is the most novel finding today. The 0600 batch of 13 resolutions includes two positions on Russell Henley that appear incorrectly settled:

- **Top 20 Finish | LAY WIN +£4.75 | Selection: Russell Henley** — should likely be a LOSS
- **Top 10 Finish | LAY WIN +£4.75 | Selection: Russell Henley** — should likely be a LOSS

External research confirms Russell Henley won the 2026 Charles Schwab Challenge at Colonial Country Club, defeating Eric Cole in a sudden-death playoff after making 4 consecutive birdies. Henley finished T1 — definitively in the top 1, 10, and 20. A LAY bet on Henley's top finish should LOSE if Henley wins the tournament.

The resolver at 06:03 UTC on June 1 logged "winner=Eric Cole (24908171)" for both Top 20 Finish and Top 10 Finish markets. Cole finished T2 after losing the playoff to Henley. The most likely explanation is a **Betfair premature settlement**: with Cole leading after 72 holes and requiring Henley to birdie the final hole to force a playoff, Betfair may have partially settled the Top N Finish markets before the playoff concluded, with Cole as the apparent tournament winner. When Henley birdied the 18th to force the playoff and then birdied the first extra hole to win, Betfair would need to void and re-settle the markets — but the Pi's resolver ran at 01:03–02:03 CT (06:03–07:03 UTC), before Betfair may have corrected the settlement.

**Immediate risk:** 2 positions totalling +£9.50 in credited LAY WINS may be incorrectly recorded. If those should be LOSS positions, the actual paper PnL is lower than stated. The exact liability per LAY LOSS depends on the odds at which the positions were placed.

**Secondary angle (if these are not Charles Schwab Challenge markets):** It remains possible that markets 1.258587518/1.258587520 are for a different golf tournament where Eric Cole genuinely won and Henley was also in the field but missed the top 20/10. Without confirming the Betfair market ID, confidence is medium. However, given the field composition (Aberg, Henley, Thomas, Fowler, MacIntyre — a mix consistent with the Charles Schwab Challenge field), the Charles Schwab Challenge hypothesis is the stronger one.

The pipeline has no built-in guard against premature Betfair settlement. The resolver trusts Betfair's settlement data unconditionally. For high-stakes events with potential for premature settlement (playoffs, in-progress events at resolver run time), adding a settlement timestamp check would mitigate this risk.
