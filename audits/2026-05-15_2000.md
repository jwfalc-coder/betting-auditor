### F001 — Root cause of back bet failure: symmetric research boost creates phantom signals

This is the most significant new finding of this slot and provides a more precise mechanistic explanation for Priority 1 (back bet failure, 20.0% win rate, n=10) than the favourite-longshot bias hypothesis in earlier audits.

Reading edge_traces.json as raw data, I found that the `boost_components` field is applied identically to **all runners in a market**, regardless of which runner the research fact pertains to. Three concrete examples:

**Rublev vs Basilashvili Moneyline:** Research found that "Andrey Rublev defeated Nikoloz Basilashvili...winning 4-3." The keywords `favorite` (+8pp) and `strong` (+5pp) were extracted and added to BOTH Rublev's claude_prob AND Basilashvili's claude_prob. Basilashvili's probability inflates from market_prob 13.24% to claude_prob 26.24% — a +13pp apparent edge — generating a BACK Basilashvili signal from evidence that Basilashvili lost the match. The sum of the two runners' claude_probs is 95% + 26.24% = 121.24%, exceeding 100% and violating basic probability axioms.

**Real Betis vs Elche Match Odds:** "Real Betis is unbeaten in the last five games against Elche" triggers `beat` (+8pp) applied to Real Betis, Elche CF, **and** Draw. All three outcomes get positive edge simultaneously. The sum of claude_probs reaches 124.01%.

**Southampton vs Middlesbrough:** "Southampton defeated Middlesbrough 2-1" gives Southampton +5pp and Middlesbrough +5pp — the losing team's probability is inflated by news of their own defeat.

The `top_boost_words` in improvement_inputs.json confirms how pervasive this is: `won` (75), `winner` (38), `strong` (83) are the most frequent triggers — all sports results vocabulary that generates positive boosts applied uniformly across a market. In any match with a clear favourite, research confirming the favourite's strength inflates the underdog's probability, creates a BACK underdog signal, and that signal loses.

The 20% back bet win rate (below coinflip at 50%) reflects this: phantom signals on underdogs are structurally biased to lose because they are generated by evidence favouring the favourite. This also explains why **all** Matchbook losses are sports back bets on match odds / moneyline — exactly the market type where opposing runners are in the same market and receive the same boost.

The favourite-longshot bias and threshold arguments in earlier audits are not wrong as structural phenomena, but the primary cause of the current 20% win rate is the symmetric boost bug, not exchange market structure.
