Recent 2025-2026 research on Kalshi (GWU/UCD Working Paper 2025) confirms the favourite-longshot bias persists in modern exchange-structured markets: low-probability contracts systematically underperform (takers lose over 60% of capital on sub-10c contracts) while high-probability contracts yield small positive returns. This supports the bot's thesis that market participants structurally overbuy longshots and underbuy favourites. The bot's strategy of laying selections its model identifies as market favourites priced too short (overbacked) is the correct direction — though note that blanket favourite-laying yields only a marginal edge; the model must detect genuine mispricing above commission to produce the 79%/53% backtest figures.

For golf outrights specifically, multi-runner Top-N markets (Top 5, Top 10) have a larger field size than binary markets, which increases the range of meaningful implied probabilities and makes favourite-overprice patterns more detectable.
