### F001 — Negative available balance: signals silently suppressed

At 06:00 UTC, the paper tracker reported a negative available balance: **Balance £175.68, Reserved £178.26, Available £-2.58**. This caused two "Top 10 Finish" positions to be skipped with the message "Skipping (insufficient balance): Top 10 Finish needs £4.95, only £-2.58 available." A third signal was independently skipped by the high-odds lay filter.

This is directly operationally significant: the bot is silently not logging paper trades because of a bookkeeping problem, not because the signals are weak. The reserved amount exceeds the actual balance, meaning some open positions are not being counted toward final trades. The most likely cause is the two stuck Scheffler positions (see F002), which have been open since at least May 10 and are permanently reserving stake amounts.

The 0600 run otherwise completed cleanly — the paper_tracker NameError (12:00 F001) had not yet manifested, and the high-odds lay filter still referenced `high_odds_min_edge` correctly at that time.

---

### F002 — Standings resolver calls team-table for golf markets; two Scheffler positions stuck

Across both the 0600 and 1000 runs, the resolution system attempted to check two season-futures positions against a **"24-team table"** for the name **"Scottie Scheffler"**. Both attempts failed: golf uses individual leaderboards, not team league tables. The "24-team table" is almost certainly a football or basketball standings structure.

Scottie Scheffler is the PGA Tour world #1. The relevant event is the **Truist Championship** (May 7-10, 2026, Quail Hollow Club, Charlotte — Cameron Young won). Scheffler's finishing position can be verified from that leaderboard. Until the positions are manually resolved or the resolver is fixed, they will persist as open, reserve stake amounts, and keep the available balance negative.

The fix has two parts: (1) immediate manual resolution of the two positions from the Truist Championship leaderboard; (2) code-level fix to route golf market resolution to a golf data source rather than a team table.

### F003 — Dashboard trade count fell 33 between 0600 and 1000 runs

The static dashboard reported **132 trades** at 0600 and **99 trades** at 1000. Signals (63→76) and markets (483→623) increased normally between runs, making the 33-trade drop anomalous. The most likely cause is the paper_tracker NameError crash at 1000 — the crash occurs mid-execution inside `log_paper_trades()`, before the atomic write completes. If partial state was written to disk, the dashboard's aggregation of trade records would reflect a reduced count.

The daily_snapshot.json reports 79 resolved trades (not 99 or 132), confirming the dashboard counts all positions (open + resolved), not just resolved ones. If 33 open position records were dropped by the crash, the downstream portfolio state — and any live trading decisions based on it — would be compromised. This is a direct consequence of the unpatched paper_tracker NameError (12:00 F001).

### F004 — 7-9pp distribution review overdue: 33.3% win rate on 27 resolved observations

The "REVIEW DUE" banner for the 7-9pp distribution appeared in **both** today's log runs (0600 and 1000), printed twice in each. The review trigger threshold of 12 resolved entries was crossed well before today. With 27 resolved observations and a 33.3% win rate (9/27), this band is performing at roughly two-thirds of the coinflip baseline — significantly worse than both the 10-11pp distribution (62.2%, n=37) and 12pp+ distribution (50.3%, n=171).

This data point strengthens the 12:00 audit's F005 argument for raising the minimum trade threshold from 10pp to 12pp. The 7-9pp distribution win rate at 33.3% confirms that the current model's edge estimates are unreliable below 10pp, and the 10-11pp trade data (-£57.62, 50.0% win rate, n=16) shows the noise extends up to 12pp. The operator should run `python distribution_logger.py report` and then `prompts/prompt_review_v1.md` as the system has been requesting.
